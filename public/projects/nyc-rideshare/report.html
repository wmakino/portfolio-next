Business Understanding

A new ride-share platform intends to enter the NYC market, Empire. To compete against the big players (notably Uber and Lyft), it needs to understand demand patterns, base performance, and vehicle deployment strategies across time. The NYC TLC’s FHV Base Aggregate Report provides monthly totals of dispatched trips, dispatched shared trips, and unique dispatched vehicles by base, which can be used to study market dynamics and estimate demand, most profitable segments, amount of vehicles needed for operation.

Business Questions

1. How many unique vehicles are required to meet a target share of monthly trips?

2. What natural groupings exist among NYC ride-share companies when considering their monthly trips and active vehicles?

3. Can we distinguish the big players (Uber/Lyft) from smaller ones using only aggregate data?

Apparently with the given data, it is really easy to tell apart Uber, Lyft and Others using Decision Trees. It used concepts that were detected previously in this analysis: low number of total trips has to be Others, since the big players have a bigger volume of trips, then to split between the two dominant it’s just a case of looking at trips per vehicle, which has came previously that Uber tends to have much more trips per unique vehicle in average, making it very easy to split them apart.    The magic numbers are:  Less than 1.5M total trips = Others
More or equal to 1.5M total trips = Uber or Lyft    Less than 141 trips per vehicle: Lyft
More or equal to 141 trips per vehicle: Uber

Summary & Conclusion

Overall, all the methods worked very well.
Linear regression showed a strong correlation between total trips and unique vehicles and showed that for each new vehicle in the fleet, there is an increase of ~160 new trips per month.
K-Means had a good result, but a very predictable and logic one. The clustering it came up with was splitting the data in 2: low and high volume.
Decision Tree also ended up having an easy task and 100% correctly splitting the monthly aggregates into Uber, Lyft and Others.
